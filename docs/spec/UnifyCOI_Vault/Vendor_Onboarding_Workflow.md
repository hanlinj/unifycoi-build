# Screen / Workflow: Vendor Onboarding

> The vendor-facing flow the [[Invite Vendor Workflow]] hands off to. **Mobile-first, tokenized, no login.** A tradesperson onboards from their phone between jobsites and should never see compliance jargon.
> Verb-style workflow doc; follows the shape of [[Invite Vendor Workflow]]. `[[wikilinks]]` point to related docs.

## Scenario

Acme Plumbing's office manager gets a text/email: *"Storage Star needs a few documents before Acme can start work — it takes about five minutes."* They tap the link on their phone. No account, no password. UnifyCOI greets them by name and company, explains in plain language that three things are needed — proof of insurance, a W-9, and bank details for fast payment — and why. They photograph their COI; the system reads it, shows the expiration date it found, and asks them to confirm it's right. They snap the W-9, enter ACH details behind a clear "this is encrypted and only authorized staff can see it" reassurance, review a summary, and submit. They're told what happens next and that they'll hear back if anything's needed. Done from a truck cab.

## Preconditions

- The vendor arrived via the **tokenized link** generated by [[Invite Vendor Workflow]]. The token identifies the vendor/invite, is single-purpose, and expires.
- A vendor record already exists at status **Invited/Pending** (created by the invite). Opening the link moves it to **Onboarding**.
- **No authentication.** The token *is* the credential. The vendor never creates an account or logs in.
- The vendor's company name, contact name, and the set of required documents are known from the invite and the resolved requirements (see [[Document Verification]] for how requirements resolve per trade × location).

> **Decision to confirm:** Token lifetime and resume. Default: the link is valid for **14 days**, is **single-vendor** (not shareable to approve others), and supports **save-and-resume** — reopening the same link returns the vendor to where they left off rather than restarting. A magic re-send (new token) is available if it expires. Confirm the window.

## What the vendor provides

The exact set is driven by the **effective requirements** for this vendor's trade and assigned location(s) — see [[Requirements Configuration]]. The common set:

| Document | Always required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **COI** (Certificate of Insurance) | **Yes** (platform floor) | The core artifact. Carries one or more policies (GL, auto, workers' comp, umbrella) each with its own limits and **expiration date**. Drives most of [[Document Verification]]. |
| **W-9** | **Yes** (platform floor) | Tax identification for payment setup. |
| **ACH / banking** | Configurable | Sensitive (account + routing, sometimes a voided check). Collected with extra care — see Sensitive data below. *(Decision to confirm: required vs. optional-at-onboarding — default required, since the invite promises "prompt payment.")* |
| **License / cert** | Per trade | e.g. electricians, sometimes only certain trades (set as a trade override in [[Requirements Configuration]]). |

> Plain-language framing only. The vendor sees *"proof of insurance,"* not *"ACORD 25 with additional-insured endorsement and waiver of subrogation."* What's actually verified is the platform's job, not theirs (MISSION: vendors never need to understand compliance terminology).

## Steps (happy path)

1. **Open link.** Vendor taps the tokenized link. Status moves Invited/Pending → **Onboarding**. A short welcome: who's asking (the operator), what's needed, roughly how long.
2. **Why this matters.** One screen, plain language: the three things and the reason ("so you can be paid quickly and start work"). No insurance vocabulary.
3. **Upload COI.** Vendor photographs or uploads their certificate. [[Document Verification]] extracts the data immediately (carrier, policy types, limits, named insured, **expiration date(s)**).
4. **Confirm expiration date(s).** The system shows the **expiration date(s) it read** and asks the vendor to confirm or correct each. This is a hard, deliberate step — see Expiration gate below. (It also catches extraction errors and creates a vendor attestation.)
5. **Upload W-9.** Photograph/upload. Light extraction (name, TIN) for the record.
6. **Provide ACH / banking.** Behind a clear security reassurance (see Sensitive data). Entered or uploaded (voided check).
7. **Upload license/cert** *(only if required for this trade).*
8. **Review summary.** A plain-language recap of everything provided and the confirmed expiration date(s). Vendor can go back and fix any item.
9. **Submit.** Status moves Onboarding → **Under Review**. [[Document Verification]] runs the full check against the effective requirements.
10. **Confirmation screen.** "You're all set — Storage Star will review and reach out if anything's needed. You don't need to do anything else right now." Plain, reassuring, no compliance status exposed.

## Expiration gate (objective — handled here, never sent to the Admin)

Expiration is **not open to interpretation**: a policy is either current or it isn't. So it's caught at the vendor step, not in the Admin's review queue.

- After COI upload, the system extracts each required policy's **expiration date** and asks the vendor to **confirm** it.
- If a confirmed expiration is **in the past**, the vendor is told plainly and immediately — *"This certificate expired on [date]. Please upload a current one to continue"* — and **cannot proceed** with that document. The expired doc **never reaches the Admin**; it's not a judgment call.
- This keeps objectively-dead documents out of human review entirely (MISSION: minimize human chase-work; exception-based operations).
- Every other kind of shortfall — a limit below requirement, a missing endorsement, a wrong named insured — is **not** gated here. Those may warrant Admin judgment or an exception, so they flow to the Admin flagged via [[Document Verification]].

> **Decision to confirm:** Expiring-very-soon at onboarding. A COI that's valid today but expires in, say, 5 days passes the gate but should arguably be flagged. Default: **allow through, flag to Admin** (don't block the vendor on something still technically valid); the future renewal chase picks it up. Confirm the threshold and whether it blocks or merely flags.

## Sensitive data (ACH / banking / tax)

MISSION #9 — secure by default. The vendor is wary of sending banking info to people they don't know; minimize that friction by making safety visible.

- ACH and W-9 collection screens carry an explicit, plain reassurance: encrypted, stored securely, visible only to **Admins** with a legitimate need — **not** to the store manager who sent the invite (see [[Roles & Permissions]] and [[Document Storage & Security]]).
- Minimize exposure: the store manager who sent the invite does **not** see banking details. Sensitive fields are masked in the record except to Admins.
- Masking and access rules are defined in [[Document Storage & Security]] (Sensitive data is **Admin-only** — no finance/AP role).

## Edge & error states

- **Expired policy on upload:** hard stop on that document, vendor must upload a current one (see Expiration gate). Never escalates to the Admin.
- **Unreadable / wrong document:** extraction fails or the doc clearly isn't what was asked (a random photo, an illegible scan). Ask the vendor to retake/re-upload with a hint ("make sure all four corners are visible"). *(Decision to confirm: is "wrong document type" an objective vendor-step bounce like expiration, or does it go to the Admin flagged? Default: bounce obvious mismatches to the vendor; route anything ambiguous to the Admin.)*
- **Partial submission / drop-off:** the vendor leaves mid-flow. Save-and-resume preserves progress; reopening the token returns them to the next unfinished step. A reminder cadence nudges them (ties to the "minimize human chase-work" principle — the system follows up, not the manager). *(Confirm cadence.)*
- **Token expired:** the link no longer works; show a friendly "this link expired — ask your contact to resend" and notify the inviter so they can re-send a fresh token from the [[Manager Home Dashboard]].
- **Already submitted:** reopening a completed token shows a read-only "you're all set" state, not the form again.
- **Vendor disputes a requirement** ("why do you need my bank info?"): inline plain-language explanation; no live chat. Communication exists only to support the workflow (MISSION non-goal: not a communication platform).
- **Multiple policies, mixed validity:** if the COI carries several policies and only one is expired, the gate fires on the expired one; the vendor confirms the rest. *(Confirm: block submission until all required policies are current, vs. accept partial and flag — default: block, since a required-but-expired policy is a hard fail.)*

## End state

- All required documents are submitted and the vendor has confirmed the expiration date(s).
- Vendor status = **Under Review** (shared vocabulary below).
- [[Document Verification]] runs against the effective requirements for the vendor's trade × location(s).
- Outcomes route per [[Document Verification]]: clean → Admin review-and-approve queue; deficiencies (limits/endorsements/named-insured/low-confidence) → Admin, flagged; expiration was already handled here.
- The vendor sees a neutral, reassuring confirmation — **no internal compliance status is exposed to them**. They're only contacted again if a correction is needed.

## Shared status vocabulary

Use these exact values across all workflows (defined in [[Invite Vendor Workflow]], referenced everywhere):
`Invited/Pending → Onboarding → Under Review → Approved → Expired → Non-Compliant`

> **Status sub-state (canonical):** when the Admin requests a correction (see [[Document Verification]] → deficiency loop), the vendor re-enters a resubmission state surfaced as **Onboarding** with an **Action Needed** flag — not a new top-level status. Canonicalized in [[Status Vocabulary]].

## Vendor portal scope (Segment 5 — absorbed here)

Segment 5 ("the vendor-facing portal") has **no standalone surface — by design.** The entire vendor experience is **this tokenized, no-login flow**, reused for renewals and corrections. There is deliberately no vendor account, dashboard, or login (MISSION: mobile-first, no login; vendors never see compliance internals). The Segment 5 checklist items all live here:

- **Tokenized entry (no account)** — Preconditions above + the tokenized-access model in [[Document Storage & Security]] (single-purpose, scoped, expiring; the token *is* the credential).
- **Guided upload (COI / W-9 / ACH)** — the Steps above.
- **Resubmission on deficiency** — the **Action Needed** resubmission state (Steps + status note) and the [[Document Verification]] deficiency loop; renewals reuse this same flow via [[Renewal & Expiration Chase]].
- **"What do I still need to do" view** — the guided flow itself *is* the status view: **save-and-resume** returns the vendor to the next unfinished step, and the confirmation screen / any correction request state exactly what's next. There is no separate persistent status page — and none is possible across tenants, since a token is single-tenant and single-purpose.

### Multi-client / multi-tenant vendors *(resolves the Segment 5 open question)*

A vendor working for two operators (e.g. Acme Storage and New Age Storage) is **onboarded once per client, not once globally.** Vendors are **per-tenant isolated** (locked — [[Platform Systems & Tenancy]], [[Document Storage & Security]]): a vendor serving both operators exists as **two separate vendor records with two separate document sets**, and onboards/verifies independently in each. **Collect-once applies *within* a tenant** — reuse across that operator's locations ([[Add Existing Vendor to Locations]]) — **never across tenants.** So if New Age invites a vendor already onboarded for Acme, they **re-submit** for New Age; there is no cross-tenant document reuse and no single cross-tenant vendor view.

> Why: the operators are distinct businesses with distinct requirements, and a vendor's documents — including Sensitive ACH/TIN — must never leak between tenants. Re-collection per tenant is the safe, isolated posture (MISSION #9 secure by default).
